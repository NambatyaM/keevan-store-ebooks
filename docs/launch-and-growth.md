# Launch & Growth Strategy — Keevan Store

How to introduce Keevan Store to the world, get people to know and love it, and grow sustainably.

---

## Phase 1: The Soft Launch (Weeks 1–3)

**Goal:** 10 active creators making sales. Validate the flow. Get testimonials.

### Step 1: Recruit Your First 10 Creators Manually

Do not run ads yet. Hand-pick your first creators.

**Who to target:**
- 2–3 authors you know personally (or can reach via a mutual contact)
- 2–3 university lecturers who sell notes
- 2–3 creators from Facebook groups you've already joined
- 1–2 friends or family who create digital content
- 1–2 people who have previously asked you about selling online

**How to recruit them:**
- Reach out personally via WhatsApp or direct message
- Offer to set up their store for them (do it together on a call)
- Ask for nothing in return except honest feedback
- Waive the 10% commission for the first 30 days (or make it 5%)

**Your pitch to the first 10:**
> "I built a platform for creators to sell digital products. I'm looking for 10 people to try it before we launch publicly. I'll set up your store for you personally. You keep everything you earn. All I ask is honest feedback."

### Step 2: Hand-hold Each Creator to Their First Sale

For each of the first 10 creators:
1. Help them upload their product and set a price
2. Help them write a post announcing their store on Facebook and WhatsApp
3. Have them share the link with 10–20 friends/followers
4. If a sale doesn't come in 3 days, ask a friend to buy it (reimburse them) to get the first sale dopamine hit

**Why this matters:** A creator who makes a sale in their first week is 10x more likely to keep using the platform than one who uploads and hears nothing back.

### Step 3: Collect Testimonials and Screenshots

After each first sale:
- Ask the creator: "How does it feel to make your first sale?"
- Save their quote as a testimonial
- Take a screenshot of their store (with permission)
- Ask if they'd be featured in a "Creator Spotlight" post

**Document everything** — you'll use these in Phase 2.

## Phase 2: The Public Launch (Weeks 4–6)

**Goal:** 50 creators. First 100 transactions. Social proof established.

### Step 4: Launch Post on LinkedIn

Write a personal post announcing Keevan Store. Use this structure:

> I spent the last [X months] building something for East African creators.
>
> [2–3 sentences about the problem — creators can't easily sell digital products with mobile money]
>
> Keevan Store lets authors, educators, and creators create a storefront, upload their work, and share a direct link. Buyers pay with MTN or Airtel money. Creators keep 90%.
>
> We already have [X] creators using it, including a lecturer selling course notes and an author selling e-books.
>
> [Quote from a creator]
>
> If you're a creator or know someone who should try it: keevanstore.in

**Publish on:**
- Your personal LinkedIn (will get 10x more reach than a company page)
- Keevan Store company page
- Cross-post to Facebook

### Step 5: Facebook Launch

**In your personal Facebook:**
> I built something I'm proud of and want to share it with you all.
>
> Keevan Store helps Ugandan creators sell digital products online — e-books, course notes, study guides, templates. You create a store, upload your file, and share a link. Buyers pay with mobile money. You keep 90%.
>
> If you're a creator, try it for free: keevanstore.in
>
> If you know someone who creates digital content, tag them in the comments!

**In your target Facebook Groups:**
Share the relevant version of the post (see outreach-messages.md) in each group. Don't post the same thing everywhere — tailor it to each group's audience.

**Ask your first 10 creators to share:**
> Hey [Creator], I just launched Keevan Store publicly. If you'd be willing to share your store link on your Facebook timeline, I'd really appreciate it. Here's a post you can copy-paste if you want:
>
> "I'm now selling my [product] on Keevan Store! You can buy directly with MTN/Airtel money and download instantly. Check out my store here: [link]"

### Step 6: Get Your First Press / Media Coverage

**Target small local media first:**
- Campus TV/radio stations (if you have university connections)
- Local business blogs
- Tech blogs in Uganda/Uganda (Digest Africa, Tech-ish, PC Tech Magazine)
- Creator/influencer podcasts

**Press pitch template:**
> Hi [Journalist Name],
>
> I'm the founder of Keevan Store — a new platform that helps East African creators sell digital products directly to their audience using mobile money.
>
> The problem we solve: global platforms like Gumroad and Payhip don't support East African mobile money (MTN, Airtel, M-Pesa). Creators in Uganda have no way to accept payments for digital products without expensive workarounds.
>
> Keevan Store is live now with [X] creators and [X] products. Lecturer at [University] used it to sell [X] copies of [product].
>
> Would you be interested in covering this?
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

### Step 7: Run First Small Facebook Ad

Budget: $50 for 7 days. Target Uganda, ages 22–45, interests in writing, education, e-books, teaching.

Create 2 ad variants:
- **Ad A:** Video screen recording of setting up a store in 60 seconds
- **Ad B:** Testimonial quote from a creator with their photo

## Phase 3: Building Love & Word of Mouth (Months 2–3)

**Goal:** Creators love the platform and tell others about it without being asked.

### Step 8: Over-Deliver on Support

When a creator joins:
- Send a welcome message within 1 hour
- Check in after 3 days: "How's it going?"
- Check in after first sale: "Congrats! How can I help you get another?"
- Answer all messages within 24 hours (within 1 hour during business hours)

**People remember how you made them feel.** A founder who personally messages new users creates loyalty that no amount of advertising can buy.

### Step 9: Create a Creator Community

Start a WhatsApp group or Facebook Group for Keevan Store creators.

**Name:** "Keevan Store Creators — East Africa"
**Purpose:** Community support, tips, sharing wins, feedback

**Keep it active:**
- Welcome every new creator with a post
- Share tips weekly (e.g., "How to write product descriptions that sell")
- Celebrate milestones (first sale, 10th sale, first withdrawal)
- Ask for feedback and feature requests
- Let creators help each other

**This community becomes your moat.** When creators build relationships with each other on Keevan Store, they won't leave.

### Step 10: The Referral Loop

When a creator makes their first sale, ask them:

> "If you know another creator who should be on Keevan Store, send them this link. When they make their first sale, I'll send you a small thank-you."

**Incentive options:**
- 5,000 UGX mobile money per referred creator who makes a sale
- Or: "I'll feature your store in our next newsletter/social post"
- Or: "I'll create a custom banner for your store"

### Step 11: Ship Features Based on Feedback

Your early creators will tell you what's missing. Listen carefully.

**Common early requests might be:**
- Multiple product images / gallery
- Different file formats per product
- Discount codes
- Email notifications to creator on sale
- Mobile app

Ship the most-requested features, announce them publicly, and thank the creator who suggested it. This proves you listen.

## Phase 4: Scaling (Months 4–6)

**Goal:** 100+ creators, consistent 10+ sales/week, path to sustainability.

### Step 12: Content Flywheel

At this point, you have enough creators and sales to create a steady stream of content:
- Creator success stories (interview a creator, write a case study)
- Monthly earnings report (transparent: "Keevan Store processed X UGX this month")
- Educational content (how to promote, how to price, how to grow)
- Industry content (state of digital publishing, creator economy in Africa)

Each piece of content:
1. Published on LinkedIn + Facebook
2. Posted in relevant groups
3. Added to the Keevan Store blog
4. Shared in the creator community

### Step 13: Strategic Partnerships

**Partner with organizations that already have creator networks:**
- University faculties (offer free stores to lecturers)
- Writer associations (Uganda Women Writers Association, Uganda Publishers Association)
- Teacher unions and associations
- Book clubs (get them to recommend Keevan Store to authors they follow)

**Partnership pitch:**
> Hi [Organization],
>
> I run Keevan Store, a platform that helps East African creators sell digital products online. I noticed your organization works with [authors/teachers/creators].
>
> I'd love to offer your members free Keevan Store accounts with a special setup guide. No cost to you or your members. It gives your members a new way to monetize their work.
>
> Would you be open to a quick chat?

### Step 14: Paid Ads at Scale

Once you have social proof (testimonials, case studies, sales data), scale ads:
- Facebook Ads: $300–$500/month
- Target: Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania
- Test different ad creatives (video demos, testimonials, carousel of creator stores)

### Step 15: Expand Geographically

Once Uganda is working well:
- Open for Kenyan creators (M-Pesa payments via Pesapal)
- Open for Tanzanian creators
- Open for Rwandan creators

Each new country requires:
- Localized marketing (each country has different mobile money providers, different creator communities)
- Local testimonials (Keevan Store works in Kenya too!)
- Local Facebook groups and LinkedIn connections

## Key Principles for Building Love

### 1. Solve One Problem Really Well

Do not try to be everything. Keevan Store's one job: **Let creators sell digital products and get paid via mobile money.** Everything else (analytics, design, etc.) supports that core job.

### 2. Be Obsessed with the Creator's Success

When a creator succeeds on Keevan Store, they tell everyone. When they fail (no sales, confusing experience), they also tell everyone.

Track creator success metrics:
- % of creators who upload a product within 7 days of signing up
- % of creators who make a first sale within 30 days
- Average time from signup to first sale

If any of these numbers are low, fix the onboarding flow before adding new features.

### 3. Move Slowly and Don't Break Things

You don't need to ship fast. You need to ship right. A payment platform that fails during a transaction loses trust forever.

Test everything. Especially payment flows. If in doubt, run it in sandbox first.

### 4. Act Small While You're Small

Your advantage over big competitors is that you can personally know every creator. Do not automate what you can personalize.

- Personal welcome messages
- Personal check-ins
- Personal responses to every piece of feedback

Do this until you literally cannot (500+ creators). Then automate only what you must.

### 5. Build in Public

Share what you're learning:
- "This week, 3 creators made their first sale. Here's what they did differently."
- "I asked creators what feature they want most. Here are the results."
- "Keevan Store processed X transactions this month. Here's the breakdown."

Building in public builds trust, attracts creators who want to be part of the journey, and makes people root for you.
