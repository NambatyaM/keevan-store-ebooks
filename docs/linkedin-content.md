## LinkedIn Profile Setup Guide

Your LinkedIn profile is the #1 asset for attracting creators. Here's how to set it up to attract authors, educators, and digital creators in East Africa.

### 1. Headline (120 characters)

Make it clear what you do AND who you help.

```
Building Keevan Store | Helping East African Creators Sell Digital Products Online
```

OR

```
Founder @ Keevan Store | Empowering Authors & Educators in Uganda to Monetize Their Work
```

OR

```
I help East African creators accept mobile money payments and sell e-books, notes & guides online
```

### 2. About Section

Use this structure:

**Paragraph 1 — Who you help and what you do:**
> I founded Keevan Store because East African creators deserve a simple way to sell digital products without international platforms that don't support mobile money.
>
> Keevan Store lets authors, educators, and digital creators create their own storefront, upload their work, and share a direct link. Buyers pay with MTN or Airtel mobile money (or card) and get instant download. Creators keep 90% of every sale.

**Paragraph 2 — The problem you solve:**
> Before Keevan Store, East African creators had to choose between chasing payments on WhatsApp, using expensive international platforms with USD pricing, or simply not selling online at all. We exist to make digital commerce work for Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, and beyond.

**Paragraph 3 — Proof (even if early):**
> We've already helped [X] creators set up stores and process [X] transactions. Creators on our platform range from university lecturers selling course notes to authors publishing e-books.

**Paragraph 4 — Call to action:**
> If you're a creator looking to sell your work — or if you know someone who should — let's connect. Keevan Store is free to join at keevanstore.in.

### 3. Featured Section

Pin these:
- A post announcing the platform launch
- A creator testimonial or success story (even a simple screenshot of a sale or feedback)
- A link to your own Keevan Store with a sample free product (shows you use your own product)

### 4. Experience Section

**Current Role:**
- Title: Founder, Keevan Store
- Description: 2–3 sentences about what Keevan Store does
- Media: Screenshot of the platform or a short demo video

### 5. Skills to Add

Add these skills so people searching for them find you:
- Digital Publishing
- E-commerce
- Creator Economy
- Mobile Money
- Educational Technology (EdTech)
- Payment Integration
- Digital Products
- Content Monetization
- East African Markets
- Entrepreneurship

### 6. Activity Guidelines

- Post 3–4 times per week (mix of posts and engagement)
- Engage with 10+ posts from your target audience daily (meaningful comments, not just "Great post!")
- Send 15–20 connection requests per week to your target audience
- Accept all connection requests (unless spam)

## LinkedIn Posts (Short-form Content)

### Category 1: Thought Leadership & Industry Insights

**Post 1 — The Opportunity:**
> The creator economy is booming globally. But most platforms ignore one thing: mobile money.
>
> In East Africa, over 60% of digital payments happen via mobile money (M-Pesa, MTN, Airtel). Yet creators are told to use Stripe, PayPal, or Gumroad — none of which work with Ugandan mobile money.
>
> That's a gap. And gaps are opportunities.
>
> Keevan Store exists to fill that gap. We help East African authors, educators, and creators sell digital products and accept mobile money payments directly.
>
> No USD conversion. No international fees. No account required for buyers.
>
> If you're an East African creator, you deserve a platform that works for your market.

**Post 2 — The Status Quo Problem:**
> A university lecturer in Kampala creates excellent course notes. Students ask to buy them.
>
> Current options:
> 1. Send via WhatsApp — track payments manually, chase defaulters
> 2. Use PayPal — students don't have it, USD conversion loses value
> 3. Bank transfer — slow, inconvenient for small amounts
> 4. Print — costly, limited reach
>
> None of these are good.
>
> Keevan Store gives option 5: upload once, share a link, student pays with mobile money, download happens instantly. You keep 90%.
>
> This is what digital commerce for East Africa should look like.

**Post 3 — Why Local Matters:**
> Global platforms build for global users. That means USD pricing, US-centric payment methods, and English-first customer support.
>
> East African creators need:
> - UGX/Shilling pricing
> - Mobile money checkout
> - Local support
> - Instant delivery
>
> That's what Keevan Store is built for. Not a Stripe clone. A platform designed from the ground up for how people actually pay and sell in Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, and Rwanda.
>
> Local problems need local solutions.

### Category 2: Practical Tips for Creators

**Post 4 — How to Price:**
> If you're selling digital products in Uganda, here's a realistic pricing guide:
>
> 📄 Course notes (semester): 30,000–70,000 UGX
> 📖 E-book (full length): 25,000–50,000 UGX
> 📋 Study guide / revision: 15,000–30,000 UGX
> 🛠 Template / resource pack: 10,000–25,000 UGX
>
> At 50,000 UGX per sale, you only need 20 sales to earn 900,000 UGX (after 10% platform fee).
>
> What do you sell and what do you price it at?👇

**Post 5 — How to Start:**
> 3 steps to start selling digital products in Uganda today:
>
> 1️⃣ Create a store — sign up on Keevan Store (free, takes 2 minutes)
> 2️⃣ Upload your product — PDF, EPUB, MOBI, or ZIP (max 4MB)
> 3️⃣ Share your link — post on Facebook, WhatsApp, or send directly
>
> When someone buys, you get 90%. The money sits in your balance until you withdraw (minimum 50,000 UGX).
>
> No website needed. No technical skills. No upfront cost.
>
> Who's starting this week? 👇

**Post 6 — The PDF Problem:**
> One of the biggest problems with selling PDFs on WhatsApp:
>
> You send the file → customer says "I'll pay later" → they read it → they never pay.
>
> Keevan Store solves this by putting payment BEFORE download. The buyer pays first, the system verifies the payment against Pesapal, and only then is the download link generated.
>
> No chasing. No awkward follow-ups. No lost income.
>
> Payment-first delivery. Simple concept, massive difference.
